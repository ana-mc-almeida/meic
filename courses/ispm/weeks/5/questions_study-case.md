# Questions Study Case 5

### 1. What were the main challenges faced by the Hong Kong medical branch of the insurance company before adopting Scrum, and how did these challenges impact their business operations?

The Hong Kong medical branch faced high costs per processed claim, slow health service capabilities, and poor collaboration due to teams working in silos across local and regional offices. The lack of communication led to inefficiencies and poor customer experiences. Additionally, they had no system to track progress or measure outcomes. Some teams used Scrum mechanically, without focusing on customer value or embracing Scrum Values, resulting in limited transparency, trust, and business agility.

### 2. How did the implementation of Professional Scrum contribute to improving teamwork, transparency, and collaboration within the organization?

The implementation of Professional Scrum improved collaboration by bringing local and regional teams together and enhancing communication. Regular Scrum events increased transparency, providing visibility into progress and creating space for feedback. Knowledge-sharing sessions broke down silos and strengthened mutual understanding. As the team aligned around shared Sprint Goals, trust grew, empowering members and fostering a stronger sense of identity, enhancing teamwork and cooperation.

### 3. What specific Scrum practices and techniques were introduced to help the teams shift from a mechanical application of Scrum to a value-driven approach?

The teams adopted key Scrum practices to become more value-driven. They set clear Sprint Goals to align efforts with customer outcomes and held regular Scrum events to boost transparency and collaboration. Knowledge-sharing sessions improved cross-functional understanding, and the Definition of Done ensured quality standards. Kwan promoted self-management, empowering the team to take ownership of their work and shift from traditional task-driven methods to focusing on delivering value.

### 4. What measurable improvements and business outcomes did the company achieve after adopting Scrum, and how did these changes affect customer experience and operational efficiency?

After adopting Scrum, the company improved teamwork, transparency, and morale, with team members feeling more empowered and happier. Collaboration between local and regional teams increased, and business processes became clearer, enhancing decision-making and reducing costs. Customer experience improved with clearer communication, while operational efficiency rose through faster claim processing and better alignment with customer needs, fostering a sustainable work pace.
