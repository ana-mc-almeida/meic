# Questions Study Case 6

### 1. What were the primary challenges faced by the company that led to the decision to adopt Scrum, and how did these challenges affect their competitiveness in the secure communications market?

The company faced slow product development, inefficient workflows, and difficulty adapting to changing market demands, which hampered innovation and delayed releases. These challenges weakened its competitiveness in the secure communications market, where agility and rapid response to evolving threats were crucial. Adopting Scrum aimed to enhance collaboration, streamline processes, and accelerate time-to-market, enabling the company to regain its edge and better meet customer needs.

### 2. How did the company approach the implementation of Scrum, and what specific actions were taken to improve the understanding and application of Scrum principles across the organization?

The company adopted Scrum through a phased implementation, starting with pilot teams to refine the approach before scaling. Leadership invested in training programs, coaching sessions, and workshops to build a strong Scrum foundation. Cross-functional teams were established, daily stand-ups and sprint reviews became standard, and a culture of continuous feedback was encouraged. Tools for backlog management and transparent communication were introduced to reinforce Scrum principles company-wide.

### 3. What were some of the major impediments the company encountered during their Scrum experimentation, and how did they work to overcome these obstacles to foster collaboration between different departments?

The company faced resistance to change, unclear role expectations, and coordination challenges between departments. Some teams struggled with adapting to iterative workflows, while others hesitated to embrace transparency. To address this, leadership provided targeted coaching, clarified roles, and promoted cross-team collaboration through shared sprint goals. Regular retrospectives helped identify pain points, and Scrum Masters facilitated smoother interdepartmental communication and alignment.

### 4. Based on the retrospective insights provided in the case study, what lessons did the company learn about the impact of gradual change, trust-building, and the role of management in supporting Scrum adoption?

The company learned that gradual change was essential for long-term success, as it allowed teams to adapt without disruption. Trust-building emerged as a critical factor, requiring open communication and psychological safety for teams to embrace new ways of working. Management played a pivotal role by fostering a supportive environment, actively engaging in Scrum practices, and removing organizational barriers.
