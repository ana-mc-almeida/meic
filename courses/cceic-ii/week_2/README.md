## Assignment 1 - COMMUNICATION DEBUGGING

### Rules:

Contributes 30% to final grade. Setup: individual; written, 20 minutes; oral presentation, max 1 min. Note: Students receive a short scenario/message in class.

You must:

- Identify assumptions / possible misunderstandings (mental models, ladder of inference).
- Rewrite the message for clarity and psychological safety.
- Propose 2–3 clarification / de-escalation lines.
- Deliver a short oral explanation (maximum duration: 1 minute) summarizing: what was misunderstood, what was changed, and why the new version is safer.

#### Evaluation Criteria (0-20)

1.  Facts vs interpretations + assumptions (0–5)
    1.  Top marks look like:
        1.  You separate facts from interpretations.
        2.  You identify key assumptions, missing context, ambiguous terms.
        3.  You recognize attribution leaps.

2.  Clarity rewrite (0–7)
    1. Top marks look like:
       1. Clear purpose + context + request.
       2. Concrete next steps: who does what by when.
       3. Removes vague terms.

3) Psychological safety language (0–5)
   1. Top marks look like:
      1. Neutral tone, no blame.
      2. Acknowledges the other person (validation) without agreeing.
      3. Calm clarification questions.

4) Channel + next step (0–3)
   1. Top marks look like:
      1. You choose the right channel (chat/email/ticket/meeting) and say what happens next.

### Email message:

"I don't understand why this wasn't done. We discussed this last week. Please resolve today."

#### Ladder of inference:

- data I noticed
  - Teammate did not complete the task we discussed last week
- meaning I made
  - Teammate is not working on the task
- assumptions
  - Teammate is not prioritizing the task
- conclusion
  - I think they are not doing their job
- action I want to take
  - Ask them why they have not completed the task and if they need any support to complete it by the end of the day

#### Rewrite:

Channel: Message on teams

Hi, I noticed that the endpoint that we discussed last week is not implemented yet. I really need it for tomorrow's demo and I know that you also have a lot on your plate rn, so I wanted to check if you need any support to get it done by the end of the day. Do you have any questions about what the endpoint should receive or return? Did you encounter any issues while implementing it? I'll be working until 6pm today and I need you to make sure that it is done by then, so feel free to ping or call me if you need any help before I log off.

#### Presentation:

1. What was misunderstood:
   - The original message was vague, did not provide any context about the **task** nor the **deadline**, and had a **blaming tone** that could indicate that we were assuming that the teammate was not doing their job or not prioritizing the task.
2. What was changed:
   - The rewritten message provides **clear context** about the **task**, the **deadline**, why it is **important** to do it before the deadline, and **offers support** to the teammate. It also has a **neutral tone** and does not blame the teammate for not completing the task.
3. Why the new version is safer:
   - The new version is safer because it does not assume that the teammate is not doing their job or not prioritizing the task.
   - It acknowledges that they might have a lot on their plate and offers support to help them complete the task.
   - It also provides clear next steps and a deadline, which can help the teammate prioritize their work and avoid last-minute stress.
   - Instead of email it uses a message on teams, which allows for quicker communication and follow-up by message or call if needed.

Final grade: 13/20
